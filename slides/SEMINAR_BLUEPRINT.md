# MedRaC Scientific Seminar Blueprint

## Communication objective

By the end of the seminar, the audience should distinguish final-answer accuracy from process-level correctness, understand the Formula-Extraction-Calculation-Answer framework, and identify how Formula RAG and Python execution address different failure stages.

## Scientific structure

1. Motivation and Research Problem - slides 1-6.
2. Step-wise Evaluation Framework - slides 7-14.
3. MedRaC - slides 15-19.
4. Experiments and Results - slides 20-26.
5. Local Demo and Mini-Experiment - slides 27-29.
6. Discussion and Conclusion - slides 30-32.

The paper occupies 26 of the first 29 substantive slides. Local material is limited to method illustration and a clearly labelled ten-case comparison.

## Visual system

- 16:9 canvas; white or light-neutral background.
- Dark navy title bar; muted medium-blue secondary accents; near-black body text.
- No orange except inside unmodified paper figures.
- One scientific claim and no more than two major visual regions per slide.
- Original paper figures and table crops preserve labels, axes, legends, and colors.
- Every evidence-bearing slide includes a source note and a concise takeaway.
- Paper-reported results, local results, and interpretation are explicitly labelled.

## Main claim

> Trustworthy medical calculation requires validation of the complete Formula -> Extraction -> Calculation -> Answer process; retrieval and code execution improve different failure stages.

## Constraints

- English-only visible slide content.
- No repository-audit, dependency-installation, API-key, or debugging material in the main deck.
- No hidden chain-of-thought.
- No local step-wise metrics inferred from missing outputs.
- No clinical-deployment or statistical-significance claims.
